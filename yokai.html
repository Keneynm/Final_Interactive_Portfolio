Date: January 2021
Program: Scratch
A set of three drawings made for the same prompt: Yokai, or Japanese Spirits. I interpreted general descriptions of each Yokai and added a seasonal theme for each drawing.

Fall: A drawing of a Yokai called the Okuri Inu(Sending-Off Dog) who follows lone travelers along dark mountain pathes. It's often accompanied by Yosuzume(Night Sparrows), who fly around the traveler in eerie flocks.

Winter: A drawing of the Yogen no Tori(Prophecy Bird) who comes to humans to warn them of eminent disasters, especially diseases.

Summer: A drawing of a Nekomata(Fork Tailed Cat), a Yokai born when a cat reaches a certain grand age and splits its tail into two. These feline spirits often take on human characteristics.
